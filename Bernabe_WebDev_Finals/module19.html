The country recorded 2,996 more COVID-19 infections, bringing the total number of cases to 334,770, the Department of Health (DOH) said on Friday.
The health department also announced 1,045 new survivors, raising the tally of recoveries to 275,307. This is the highest number of survivors since Sept. 19 that is not included in the agency's "time-based recoveries" announcement every Sunday which can sometimes list over 20,000 recoveries.The death toll also jumped to 6,152 after 83 more patients succumbed to the disease.

The government has eased its COVID-19 restrictions to allow face-to-face trainings and assessments in technical vocational institutions in areas under general community quarantine. The Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) classes are covered by the new rule. Previously, in-person sessions can only be conducted in areas under the less strict modified general community quarantine. On Thursday, the Inter-Agency Task Force on Emerging Infectious Diseases approved Resolution No. 78, giving green light to face-to-face competency assessment for domestic work, caregiving, housekeeping, and other tech-voc courses which the Department of Trade and Industry allows to operate up to 50 percent assessment site capacity.
